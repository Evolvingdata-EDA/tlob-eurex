The key methodological move

A label a maker can actually learn

  • The problem — the standard label gates a move on spread + fee. At 1s almost nothing clears ~2 ticks → ~97% "flat" → degenerate to learn.
  • The insight — as a maker we never cross the spread, so drop the gate.
  • New target — sign of the next-second smoothed-mid drift → a balanced ~33/33/33 up/flat/down.
  • Framework — added label_threshold_ticks to tlob, freeing fee for the backtest.

Tick horizons post a higher headline — but read the flat column. At 1 event the target is ~82% "flat", so accuracy 0.861 / flat-F1 0.908 are inflated by the easy class; the honest read is the up/down F1 (~0.72). As the event horizon lengthens (1→5) the flat share falls toward balance (~82% → 40%) and the up/down F1 stays strong. The 5-event target is the most balanced tick target and is genuinely more predictable than 1s (0.68 vs 0.61 macro-F1) — a 5-event smoothed drift persists more than a 1-second one.

More inputs on far less data is less data-efficient, and depth carries little marginal signal at 1s. The same story repeats at the next-tick horizon (0.747 with L10 vs 0.793 with L1). Use TBBO / L1 — also the only feed available across all six instruments and the full year.

The insight, in one line

TLOB's edge over linear grows with the horizon

Next tickmechanical · imbalance solves it
+0.1 pt — a tie
1s smoothedsignal has decayed · sequence matters
+12.2 pts

A sequence model earns its keep only once the mechanical top-of-book imbalance signal decays. At the next tick the answer is already in the current book; at 1s, the past trajectory carries the information the linear model can't see.

Result 6a — the backtest

A queue-aware market-making simulation

Earn the spread

Post one clip at best bid & ask. Maker fills pay no fee; only the close-out crosses (€0.40/side).

Trade-flow L1 queue

Join the back of the queue; real aggressor volume from the tape consumes it before our clip fills.

Adverse selection

If the market trades through our stale quote we're filled underwater — the core cost being measured.

Pull the toxic side

Predict up → pull the ask, keep the bid; mirror for down; quote both when flat.

Risk controls

Hard inventory cap ±50 lots, flatten at the 17:00 Berlin close.

Baseline control

Same maker, signal off — quotes both sides always. Signal minus baseline = what the prediction adds.

With the fee fix + realistic queue, the signal cuts adverse selection and lifts P&L +29–88% over a blind maker. A flat class is essential: the 2-class next-tick signal has no "no-move" state, so the maker is forced one-sided, flips constantly, and barely trades (1,147 fills) — non-viable despite 79% directional accuracy. Still marginally negative; closing the gap needs queue priority / confidence-weighted skew.

Recap

Six takeaways

1 · The reframing works

Dropping the spread+fee gate makes the 1s direction target learnable (vs 97%-flat).

2 · TLOB > linear at 1s

+12 pts accuracy on the identical target — the clear DNN case.

3 · Edge grows with horizon

A statistical tie at the mechanical next-tick; the DNN wins once the imbalance signal decays.

4 · L1 is enough

MBP-10 depth hurts and overfits at both horizons. Top-of-book carries the signal.

5 · Micro features add ~1 pt

Small but consistent lift on 1s and 5-tick targets.

6 · Maker ~breakeven

Signal cuts adverse selection; the flat class is what makes a viable maker.
