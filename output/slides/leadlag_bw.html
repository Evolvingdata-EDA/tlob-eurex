Cross-asset lead-lag into euro government-bond futures

Do US rates, crude and equity futures lead the Eurex govvie curve — and is it tradeable?

Results — 1-minute bars & 1-second order book · July 2026

Data & method

  • Instruments. Eurex govvies — Schatz, Bobl, Bund, Buxl, BTP, OAT. Drivers — ZN (US 10Y), CL (WTI crude), FESX / ES (equity).
  • Data. Databento TBBO top-of-book (1 s mids) and OHLCV 1-minute bars. Session 08:00–17:00 Europe/Berlin. 2025-03 → 2026-06; 231 common days at 1 s.
  • Metric. C(k) = corr( driver return[t], govvie return[t+k] ). k=0 is contemporaneous; k>0 means the driver leads by k. Log-returns, winsorized 0.1 / 99.9%.
  • Tradeable leads only. Strictly-leading lags (k ≥ 1); lag 0 is same-bar co-movement and not actionable.

1-second cross-correlation, all pairs (231 days)

The cross-correlation is asymmetric: ZN and CL carry more weight on the k>0 side (driver leads) than k<0. FESX is symmetric — synchronous, not leading. The lead is concentrated within ~1 second.

When does the lead occur? Net-lead by time of session

ZN leads the govvies all session and strengthens into the US cash open (peak 16:00; US hours 1.35× the morning). CL is weaker (1.18×). FESX does not lead at any time.

Summary

  • ZN (US 10Y) and CL (WTI) lead the euro govvies; FESX / equity is synchronous, not leading.
  • The relationship is contemporaneous-dominated. A genuine lead exists only at ~1 second and has decayed by one minute.
  • Magnitude is tiny: lead correlation ≈ 0.02 (R² 0.04%), sign accuracy ≈ 50% — below transaction cost.
  • The lead concentrates in the US session (ZN peaks ~16:00, 1.35× the morning).
  • Adding ZN as a feature does not improve a TLOB directional model of BTP.
